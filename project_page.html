Transcutaneous Tracheal Electrode (TTE)

A novel, non-invasive, and cost-effective method for preventing respiratory morbidity in the ICU.

"Dysphagia post-tracheostomy constitutes a significant concern in India, impacting up to 70% of patients and leading to a mortality rate of up to 11%. This implies that one out of every ten individuals may succumb to choking or experience grave respiratory distress following the procedure. This issue is particularly accentuated in government-run facilities, especially in rural areas, where the morbidity and mortality rates are anticipated to be more pronounced."



Our solution entails the integration of a nerve activity measuring electrode into the tracheostomy apparatus, enabling the measurement of the input from the cranial nerves involved in the process of swallowing. This non-invasive technique permits surface-level measurement, and the resulting signals are transmitted through an amplification and transmission circuit to a signal classification algorithm, which effectively detects distress signals associated with dysphagia. Upon receipt of an alert at the nurse's station, prompt intervention can be taken to rescue the patient. This solution has the potential to substantially enhance patient outcomes by enabling expedited detection and intervention in cases of dysphagia.
